Communication Systems

Common Communication Systems Questions

Question 1:In digital communication, what does QAM stand for?

The answer is: Quadrature Amplitude Modulation

Question 2: What is the primary advantage of using Frequency Modulation (FM) over Amplitude Modulation (AM)?
A) FM signals require less bandwidth.
B) FM signals are less susceptible to noise.
C) FM signals have higher power efficiency.
D) FM signals are easier to demodulate.

The answer is: B) FM signals are less susceptible to noise.

Question 3: Which of the following multiplexing techniques is used in optical fiber communication?
A) Time Division Multiplexing (TDM)
B) Frequency Division Multiplexing (FDM)
C) Wavelength Division Multiplexing (WDM)
D) Code Division Multiplexing (CDM)

The answer is: C) Wavelength Division Multiplexing (WDM)

Question 4: What is the purpose of a superheterodyne receiver?
A) To amplify the received signal
B) To convert the received signal to an intermediate frequency
C) To demodulate the received signal
D) To filter out unwanted frequencies.

The answer is: B) To convert the received signal to an intermediate frequency

Question 5: Question: In a PCM system, the process of converting an analog signal into a digital signal involves which of the following steps?
A) Sampling, Quantization, and Modulation
B) Sampling, Quantization, and Coding
C) Demodulation, Quantization, and Coding
D) Modulation, Sampling, and Coding

The answer is: B) Sampling, Quantization, and Coding
